# Episode 7: Loot Modes & RPG Design

**Scene**: Contrast between a player clicking a skill tree and a player finding a legendary tome in a dungeon chest.

---

## 🎙️ Script

**[Intro]**
"Every RPG needs a balance between what a player can choose and what they must find. Today, we're looking at **Loot Modes**. This simple field in your skill definition completely changes how your modpack is played."

**[Loot Mode: 'both']**
"By default, every skill is set to `both`. This means a player can purchase it in their skill tree, AND they can find it on gear or in tomes. It’s the standard, flexible option."

**[Loot Mode: 'tome_only']**
"Next is `tome_only`. Use this for powerful skills that you want players to focus on in their tree, but never allow them to imbue onto gear. It keeps your skill tree meaningful and prevents players from 'cheating' high-level passives through gear stacking."

**[Loot Mode: 'imbue_only']**
"Finally, `imbue_only`. This is my favorite. Skills set to this **cannot** be purchased in the skill tree. They won't even show up. 
The only way to get them is to find a Skill Tome in a loot chest or buy one from a high-tier Skill Master. This turns these skills into 'Equipment Only' enchantments. It’s perfect for legendary powers that must be earned through adventure, not just clicking a menu."

**[RPG Immersion]**
"By mixing these modes, you create a layered progression. Basic skills come from the tree, intermediate skills can be found on gear, and ultimate powers are strictly 'imbue_only', forcing players out into the world to find them."

**[Outro]**
"Acquisition is everything. In our final episode, we’re wrapping up with advanced tips, expressions, and how to troubleshoot your datapack when things go wrong!"
